# App Store Listing Refresh

Last reviewed: April 23, 2026

This document captures App Store Connect copy and screenshot guidance for the
next release. It should be reviewed manually before submission.

## Positioning

Cookle is a personal recipe manager for people who repeatedly cook from their
own collection. The strongest message is not generic storage; it is continuity:
save a recipe, cook from it, log the meal, and return to the right dish later.

## Subtitle Draft

Recipes, diary, and cooking

## Promotional Text Draft

Save recipes with photos and notes, cook from an active recipe screen, log meals
in your diary, and return from widgets, notifications, Shortcuts, or Apple
Watch.

## Description Draft

Cookle keeps your cooking life organized without turning your recipes into a
generic notes folder.

Save personal recipes with ingredients, steps, categories, serving sizes,
cooking times, notes, and photos. Open a recipe while cooking and keep the
important details close until the meal is done.

Use the diary to record breakfasts, lunches, dinners, and cooking notes. Cookle
can suggest something from your recipe collection, help you return to a recent
recipe, and surface useful entry points through widgets, notifications,
Shortcuts, and Apple Watch.

Photos can come from your library, camera, or Image Playground. Optional iCloud
sync keeps your collection available across devices, while backup export,
restore, and delete-all controls help you stay in control of your data.

Cookle is built for people who want a practical recipe collection they can
actually use in the kitchen.

## Screenshot Storyboard

- Recipe library: show search, sort, categories, and a visually rich saved
  recipe.
- Recipe detail: show ingredients, steps, photos, and active cooking entry.
- Active cooking: show the cooking-focused screen and timer continuity.
- Diary: show daily meals and the top suggestion flow.
- System surfaces: show widgets, Shortcuts, and Apple Watch companion value.
- Settings trust: show iCloud sync, backup export, restore, and delete controls.

## Accessibility Metadata Draft

- VoiceOver: supported for primary recipe, diary, photo, settings, and cooking
  actions.
- Dynamic Type: supported through standard SwiftUI text, list, form, and
  navigation surfaces.
- Reduced Transparency or Reduce Motion: no custom motion-heavy flows are
  required for the primary cooking workflow.
- Widget privacy: recipe and diary widget content is marked privacy sensitive
  where supported by the system.

## What's New Guidance

- Lead with the most user-visible new capability.
- Mention backup, restore, and cooking continuity explicitly for the 3.6 release.
- Avoid generic maintenance text unless the release is truly maintenance-only.
- Keep the final copy short enough to read inside App Store update surfaces.
